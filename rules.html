Rules

What is UnitedCTF?

UnitedCTF is a cybersecurity/programming contest tailored towards students across Quebec.

Who are the Organizers?

UnitedCTF is a coalition between the following student clubs:

  • JDIS
  • DCIÉTS
  • ApplETS
  • RHUM

Who is eligible for prizes?

This competition is open for all students across Quebec. Please use your university email in order to be eligible for prizes.

Global top 3: Each university student having a valid university email address is eligible to receive a plaque in addition to a gift card.

Local top 3: If you're in the top 3 of your university but not in the global top 3, your university will take care of your prize.

Can I play in a team?

No, this is an individual CTF.

General information

  • Communication happens via our official Discord guild.
  • The competition will span across a week (September 30th at 18:30 - October 7th at 13:00 EDT).
  • Dynamic scoring is used for all challenges (i.e., the more solves a challenge gets, the less points it is worth).
  • All flags match the following regex, unless otherwise specified: ^FLAG-.+$.
  • Challenges will be released in waves, so even if you complete all challenges, stay tuned for other challenge releases.
  • Any critical information about the competition will be communicated via Discord and/or the CTFd platform.

General rules

  1. First of all, make sure to have fun!
  2. Treat everyone with respect, that includes both the organizers and your fellow contestants.
  3. DoS/DDoS attacks against the infrastructure and/or the challenges are strictly prohibited.
  4. Do not brute-force the flag submission forms.
  5. Brute-forcing challenge endpoints is not allowed, unless otherwise specified.
  6. Report any issues you encounter with the infrastructure and/or tasks directly to the organizers.
  7. UnitedCTF is meant to be played individually, collaboration is not allowed. Do not discuss challenges with other players.
  8. If you solve a challenge, submit the flag immediately, we do not tolerate flag hoarding.
  9. Usage of automatic exploitation tools and mass vulnerability scanners is strictly prohibited.
  10. Social engineering attempts against the organizers will result in a sanction.
  11. Do not share or post any flags or solutions publicly during the CTF.
  12. The flag you're looking for is under this tag, remove all digits before submitting it.
  13. Keep your solutions (screenshots, scripts, etc.) in case the organizers ask you to provide them.
  14. UnitedCTF organizers reserve the right to disqualify people suspected for breaking the rules of the CTF.
